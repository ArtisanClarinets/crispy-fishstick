# Pricing Strategy for Vantus Systems (No Care Scope)

**Strategy type:** Package Pricing (Good / Better / Best + Enterprise Expansion Path)  
**Why this structure:** Vantus is a B2B productized-services company selling high-impact implementations, not commodity subscriptions. Package pricing matches buyer expectations for scoped project delivery, allows margin protection through complexity gating, and supports premium positioning while preserving a clear entry point.

---

### Website Rebuild (Modern + Complete)

- **Price:** $15,000 starting anchor | Typical $15,000-$55,000
- **Who it's for:** SMB owners and operators with outdated websites that are losing trust and inbound lead quality.
- **What's included:** Full rebuild implementation, responsive UX, content completion support, baseline SEO, analytics instrumentation, deployment hardening, ownership handoff.
- **Price justification:** Regional competitors publish low list-floor numbers for lead generation, but real quality delivery benchmarks and national comparables support mid-five-figure projects. This tier stays accessible while preventing low-margin delivery.

### Website + CMS (Recommended)

- **Price:** $32,000 starting anchor | Typical $32,000-$125,000
- **Who it's for:** Growth-stage SMB teams that need internal publishing control, approval workflows, and operational continuity.
- **What's included:** Everything in Website Rebuild, plus content model architecture, editorial workflow setup, role-based publishing controls, and operator training.
- **Price justification:** Competitor benchmark patterns show major uplift when workflow, governance, and structured content are included. This should be the hero tier because it maps directly to recurring business value.

### Website + Business Portal

- **Price:** $90,000 starting anchor | Typical $90,000-$400,000
- **Who it's for:** Organizations replacing spreadsheet-driven processes with secure portal workflows and integrated systems.
- **What's included:** Everything in Website + CMS, plus portal architecture, RBAC baseline, audit controls, workflow implementation, and integration foundation.
- **Price justification:** Portal projects shift from marketing surface to operational software. National and enterprise benchmark patterns support six-figure starts once security and integrations are included.

### Enterprise Systems Program (Expansion Motion)

- **Price:** Contact Sales | Typical $250,000-$1,200,000 (multi-phase)
- **Who it's for:** Multi-entity organizations with cross-team workflows, high compliance pressure, and system modernization goals.
- **What's included:** Multi-phase architecture modernization, advanced integrations, governance controls, and phased rollout governance.
- **Price justification:** High-revenue positioning requires a visible enterprise path so Vantus is not framed as a local-only implementation shop.

---

### Positioning & Psychology

- **Market position:** Premium-by-proof implementation partner for SMB and lower-midmarket organizations that need enterprise-grade execution without agency bloat.
- **Psychological tactics:**
  - **Anchoring:** Enterprise Systems Program sets the upper reference point so Website + CMS feels strategically efficient, not expensive.
  - **Decoy / Hero Tier Design:** Website + CMS is positioned as highest value density for most customers.
  - **Range Framing:** Starting anchor + typical range sets expectations while preserving scope-based flexibility.
  - **Risk Reversal:** Explicit ownership handoff and documented proof artifacts reduce vendor-lock-in fear.
- **Upgrade triggers:**
  - Rebuild -> CMS: content velocity and non-technical publishing bottlenecks.
  - CMS -> Portal: workflow approvals, customer data access, and manual process overhead.
  - Portal -> Enterprise Program: multi-system integration, compliance, and scaling complexity.

### Revenue Optimization

- Use paid discovery as a conversion filter (`$5k-$20k`) and credit a portion toward build when project proceeds.
- Standardize add-on price bands (integration, migration, SSO/RBAC hardening, analytics, localization) to prevent margin leakage.
- Enforce change-order discipline from `pricing_ops.yaml` with explicit cost + timeline impact on every scope expansion.

### Biggest Pricing Risk

The largest risk is **market confusion between lead-gen “from” prices and actual enterprise delivery cost**, causing price-shock during late-stage sales. Mitigation: publish qualification criteria and complexity drivers on the pricing page, then run every opportunity through a visible scope scoring model before proposal.
