# Product Marketing Context

_Last updated: 2026-03-05_

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Enterprise-grade websites and business systems for small businesses — with ownership, performance, and proof.

**What it does:**
Vantus builds modern websites and web apps with a real backend: CMS, portals, integrations, and deployment. Clients own their stack — code, infra, credentials — with a documented handoff so they’re never trapped.

**Product category:** Premium web development + infrastructure modernization (productized services).

**Product type:** B2B service with a standardized internal platform (VSP) to deliver consistently.

**Business model:** Project fees for build + optional recurring support (Vantus Care later). Pricing is transparent, scoped, and tied to deliverables.

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** Gulf Coast small businesses (5–50 employees) in high-value verticals: healthcare clinics, legal, trades, specialty retail, B2B services.

> **Strategy Note:** This document aligns with Option A — Gulf Coast Leader. Revenue target: $500K Y1 → $1.4M Y2 → $2.2M Y3. Exit: $8-12M sale to regional MSP consolidator.

**Decision-makers:** Owner/Founder, GM/Operations, sometimes office manager.

**Primary use case:** Replace a dated website that is losing trust/leads, and add a CMS so the business can update content without begging a developer.

**Jobs to be done:**

- “Make my business look credible online and generate leads.”
- “Stop my website from being slow, broken, or embarrassing.”
- “Give me a system I own and can run without vendor lock-in.”

**Use cases:**

- Website rebuild with full content + SEO + accessibility.
- CMS + blog/events/menu/service pages.
- Appointment/quote forms, lead routing, CRM integrations.
- Customer portal (documents, invoices, status updates).
- Admin portal (inventory, scheduling, internal workflows).

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** SMB websites are often slow, outdated, unmaintainable, and disconnected from operations.

**Why alternatives fall short:**

- Cheap shops ship brochure sites with no backend or CMS.
- Expensive agencies overcharge and create lock-in.
- DIY platforms trade control for convenience.

**What it costs them:** Lost leads, lower conversion, reduced trust, operational overhead.

**Emotional tension:** Embarrassment (“our site looks cheap”), fear (“what if it breaks?”), frustration (“we can’t edit anything”).

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct (local):** small web shops delivering dated sites with minimal backend.
**Secondary:** expensive agencies selling brand + marketing retainers.
**Indirect:** DIY platforms (Squarespace/Wix) and vertical SaaS sites.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**

- Real engineering standard (security, performance, documentation).
- CMS + optional portals + integrations.
- Client ownership (accounts, code, infra, credentials).
- Proof artifacts (Lighthouse, accessibility, security headers, runbooks).

**Why customers choose us:** We reduce risk and raise trust while making their online presence actually work.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** embarrassment, slow site, can’t update content, leads dropping.
**Pull:** modern site + CMS + proof.
**Habit:** “It’s good enough.”
**Anxiety:** “Will this take forever?” “Will it break?” “Will we lose SEO?”

## Customer Language

**How they describe the problem:**

- “Our website is old and looks bad on phones.”
- “We can’t update anything without calling someone.”
- “We don’t even know who owns our domain.”

**Words to use:** ownership, control, proof, fast, secure, modern, “built to last.”
**Words to avoid:** jargon (microservices, Kubernetes) unless asked.

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** direct, calm, confident.
**Style:** plain language, evidence-first.
**Personality:** rigorous, honest, practical.
