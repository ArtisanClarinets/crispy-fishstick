# EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY_VALUATION - Vantus Care

**Version:** 1.2.0
**Last updated:** 2026-03-07
**Classification:** Confidential - internal strategy and valuation support
**Publication rule:** Do not circulate externally as a valuation statement without stronger financial and source validation.

## Purpose

This memo is an internal valuation-support document for Vantus Care.
It is intended to help leadership think about market positioning, recurring-service quality, and possible long-term value creation.
It is not a fairness opinion, investor-ready valuation report, or externally sourced market study.

## Evidence policy

This memo contains a mix of:

- internal operating assumptions,
- management views,
- directional market observations,
- and limited external reference points.

Any statement used outside internal planning should be re-validated with a dated source, financial support, or audited operating evidence.

## Executive view

Vantus Care may become a high-quality recurring support layer if it continues to strengthen three things:

1. ownership-based commercial clarity,
2. disciplined delivery and documentation,
3. tighter linkage between project delivery and recurring support.

The strategic value in Care is not that it is already a scaled MSP platform.
The strategic value is that it can become a differentiated recurring layer attached to the broader Vantus build offer.

## What supports long-term value creation

### 1. Recurring revenue quality

Care is designed as a recurring operational layer with defined scope, reporting rhythm, and support boundaries.
That is generally more valuable than undifferentiated hourly support if it is packaged, retained, and renewed consistently.

### 2. Ownership-based positioning

Vantus has a clearer ownership story than many support providers:

- client-owned accounts,
- client-owned hardware,
- clearer offboarding rights,
- no hardware leasing model.

This may improve buyer trust and reduce lock-in concerns.

### 3. Operational discipline

The Care suite now includes:

- a controlled SOP library,
- templates and checklists,
- a pricing baseline,
- commercial support documents,
- and build-to-care alignment documents.

That documentation does not guarantee scale by itself, but it is a useful maturity signal.

### 4. Strategic fit with the enterprise pack

Care now fits the governing Vantus strategy more cleanly:

- project delivery first,
- recurring support second,
- governance and continuity as the follow-on value layer.

This sequencing is more believable than treating Care as a standalone large-scale MSP from day one.

## Current valuation stance

A responsible current view is to treat any large future valuation as an upside scenario, not as a present fact.

Use three bands for internal planning:

| Scenario | Meaning | Evidence standard |
|---|---|---|
| Cautious | Value based on early recurring discipline and modest scale | Mostly internal proof required |
| Base | Value supported by sustained recurring revenue quality and retention | Internal proof plus better benchmark support |
| Upside | Value supported by real scale, recurring quality, leadership depth, and stronger market position | Strong internal and external evidence required |

## What would need to be true for premium outcomes

A premium long-term valuation outcome would likely require evidence of:

- durable recurring revenue growth,
- strong retention,
- disciplined gross margin,
- leadership depth beyond the founder,
- a repeatable handoff from build projects into Care,
- stronger external proof of market differentiation,
- and a cleaner benchmark story tied to real operating results.

## Strategic strengths that may support value

Based on current internal review, Vantus Care appears directionally stronger in these areas:

- ownership and portability,
- recurring scope clarity,
- handoff and documentation discipline,
- no-leasing commercial posture,
- alignment with a broader project-led offer ladder.

These are useful internal strengths, but they should be described as directional advantages unless externally verified.

## Risks that limit valuation confidence today

### Commercial risk
- recurring scope could blur into project work if sales discipline slips
- discounting could weaken recurring economics if not tightly governed

### Operating risk
- documentation quality does not guarantee staffing capacity or service consistency at scale
- benchmark and reporting claims still need strong evidence if used externally

### Market risk
- regional MSP comparisons are difficult to prove with precision
- private-company peer data is limited and often not directly comparable

### Concentration risk
- if Care remains founder-dependent or overly concentrated in a narrow client base, valuation support weakens

## What to measure next

If leadership wants a stronger future valuation memo, track and retain evidence for:

- recurring revenue by Care tier,
- gross margin by tier,
- logo retention and dollar retention,
- proposal-to-close conversion,
- build-to-care conversion rate,
- average response performance by severity,
- documentation freshness and offboarding readiness,
- customer concentration,
- client lifetime value and acquisition efficiency.

## Recommended internal conclusion

The strongest current conclusion is:

Vantus Care is becoming a more credible recurring-services platform because its documentation, pricing, commercial structure, and enterprise-pack alignment are improving.
It may support meaningful long-term value creation if Vantus can prove recurring quality, retention, margins, and disciplined growth over time.

## Usage rule

Do not present a fixed valuation target from this memo as a present fact.
Use this document as an internal strategy support piece until stronger financial and market evidence exists.
