# CARE_QUOTE_ASSUMPTIONS_LIBRARY

**Status:** Active baseline
**Last updated:** 2026-03-07

## How to use this document

Use these assumptions in Care quotes and service orders to keep recurring scope separate from project work and to prevent hidden delivery obligations.
Add only the assumptions that materially affect the quoted service.

## Baseline commercial assumptions

- Pricing assumes a steady-state environment after project delivery or an approved transition-in period.
- Pricing assumes recurring operational support, not transformation work.
- Included recurring effort does not roll over unless expressly stated in writing.

## Environment assumptions

- The Client maintains required vendor licensing, subscriptions, and supported product versions.
- The Client has named business and technical contacts available for decisions and approvals.
- The Client controls its own tenant, cloud, vendor, and domain accounts.

## Scope boundary assumptions

- Routine service requests and minor changes are included only within the purchased package limits.
- Net-new integrations, migrations, rebuilds, major data cleanup, custom reporting, and major remediation are separate projects.
- Emergency work outside the support window is excluded unless specifically purchased.

## Third-party assumptions

- Third-party vendor cooperation is assumed but not guaranteed.
- Third-party outages, vendor delays, and product limitations may affect service timing.
- Third-party fees are excluded unless expressly included in the quote.

## Security and compliance assumptions

- Care supports operational hygiene and administration but does not guarantee compliance, legal sufficiency, audit readiness, or certification outcomes.
- Policy drafting, legal review, audit response, and control attestation are excluded unless separately scoped.

## Hardware and asset assumptions

- Quotes do not include hardware leasing, financing, or bundled device subscriptions.
- Hardware procurement support, if included, supports outright purchase only.

## Offboarding assumptions

- The Client retains ownership of systems, data, accounts, licenses, and credentials.
- Standard offboarding includes reasonable transition support, not major reconstruction of undocumented history.

## Pricing adjustment triggers

Pricing may need revision if any of the following materially exceed the approved assumptions:

- supported users,
- entities or locations,
- systems or vendors,
- ticket volume,
- support hours,
- platform complexity,
- reporting or governance depth.

## Example assumption bullets

- Assumes no major platform reconfiguration is required during the term.
- Assumes the Client provides timely approvals and access.
- Assumes recurring work remains within normal administrative complexity.
- Assumes project-based work will be separately quoted.
