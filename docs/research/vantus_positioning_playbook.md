## Core Positioning Pillars

### Pillar 1: Transparent Pricing
**Tagline:** *"No surprises. No hidden fees. No "contact us" games."*

**The Problem:**  
Every major Gulf Coast MSP hides pricing behind lead capture forms. Prospects can't self-qualify, creating friction and distrust.

**The Vantus Solution:**  
We publish starting prices for core services. Prospects know if we're in budget before they ever talk to us. This pre-qualifies leads AND builds trust from first impression.

**Proof Points:**
- Public pricing page with "Starting At" baselines
- Server Planner tool shows real cost projections
- No mandatory "discovery call" for basic information

---

### Pillar 2: Independent Governance Infrastructure
**Tagline:** *"Your infrastructure. Your choice. Your exit plan."*

**The Problem:**  
Competitors push Azure/M365 ecosystems that create long-term vendor dependency. Exit costs are hidden until it's too late.

**The Vantus Solution:**  
We offer client-owned, self-hosted options alongside cloud services. Every engagement includes an exit plan. We win by being the best option, not the only option.

**Proof Points:**
- Documented exit strategy in every contract
- Hybrid and on-premises options available
- "Vendor Neutrality" as a stated company value

---

### Pillar 3: Proof Over Promises
**Tagline:** *"We don't claim security. We prove it."*

**The Problem:**  
Every MSP claims "security-first" but their own digital presence tells a different story. Failed security headers, slow load times, tracking cookies everywhere.

**The Vantus Solution:**  
We hold ourselves to the standards we sell. Lighthouse 95+, security.txt, CSP headers, minimal tracking. Our website IS our case study.

**Proof Points:**

- A+ on security header tests
- No unnecessary tracking cookies

---

### Pillar 4: Performance-First

# PART III: COMPETITIVE RESPONSE SCRIPTS

## Scenario-Based Responses

### Scenario 1: Price Shopping
**Prospect says:** *"Digital Boardwalk gave us a lower quote."*

**Response:**
> "I'd encourage you to look at the total picture. What's included in that quote? Are there add-ons for security, backup, or after-hours support? What happens when you want to leave—are there data migration fees? Azure exit costs? We publish our pricing because we're confident in the total value. Let me walk you through what's included and what the 5-year cost actually looks like, including exit."

---

### Scenario 2: Brand Recognition
**Prospect says:** *"We've heard of Digital Boardwalk. We haven't heard of you."*

**Response:**
> "That's fair—they've been in the market longer. But let me ask: what have you heard about their exit process? Their Azure lock-in terms? What their clients say when they try to leave? Being well-known and being the right fit aren't the same thing. We're building a reputation on transparency and results. Let me show you what that looks like in practice."

---

### Scenario 3: Certification Flex
**Prospect says:** *"Access One is SOC 2 certified."*

**Response:**
> "Good—compliance matters. Let me clarify what they have: SOC 2 Type 1 from June 2023. That's a point-in-time snapshot, not ongoing verification. Type 2 is the standard for continuous compliance, and we're building toward that. More importantly, are they local? Can they show up when you need them? Certification without presence is just paperwork. We're doing the work AND being there when it matters."

---

### Scenario 4: CMMC Urgency
**Prospect says:** *"We're a defense contractor and need CMMC. Jun Cyber is a C3PAO."*

**Response:**
> "Jun Cyber is excellent at compliance assessment—that's their lane. But assessment isn't implementation. They'll tell you what needs to change. Who actually changes it? That's where we come in. We're building CMMC 2.0 controls into our standard service offering. You don't need two vendors—you need one that does both. And we're local, which matters when the assessor wants to see your physical controls."

---

### Scenario 5: Big Company Security
**Prospect says:** *"We want enterprise-grade security. The bigger MSPs must be more secure."*

**Response:**
> "Let me test that assumption. Have you checked their security headers? Run a Lighthouse audit on their site? Digital Boardwalk's fails basic CSP tests. Access One's SOC 2 is outdated. Big doesn't mean secure—it often means legacy systems and bureaucracy. We're small enough to be agile, secure enough to prove it, and transparent enough that you can verify it yourself. Pull up securityheaders.com and test our site right now."

---

### Scenario 6: Microsoft Ecosystem
**Prospect says:** *"We're already on Microsoft 365. We need a Microsoft Partner."*

**Response:**
> "We work with Microsoft 365 every day. The question isn't whether we support it—it's whether you want a partner who only supports it. Microsoft Partners are incentivized to keep you in the ecosystem, even when there's a better option. We're vendor-neutral. If Microsoft is right for a specific need, we'll say so. If there's a better fit elsewhere, we'll say that too. You want a partner who recommends solutions, not a partner who sells products."

---

### Scenario 7: Pricing Calculator
**Prospect says:** *"VTC Tech has a pricing calculator."*

**Response:**
> "They do—but use it and tell me what happens. You still have to request a quote, right? That's not transparency, that's lead generation dressed up as helpfulness. We show actual starting prices, and they're real. No calculator-to-quote bait and switch. Plus, they're a 14-person company in Vermont. When you need on-site support, who shows up? We're here, staffed, and we don't play games with pricing."

---

### Scenario 8: Relationship/Referral
**Prospect says:** *"Our accountant uses Digital Boardwalk and recommended them."*

**Response:**
> "Referrals matter, and that's a data point. But let me ask: does your accountant have the same IT needs you do? Are they subject to the same compliance requirements? Do they have the same growth trajectory? What works for a 10-person accounting firm might not scale for your business. And if they ever want to leave, what does that process look like? Ask them about their exit options. Then let's talk about what a partnership with exit-built-in looks like."

# PART IV: PRICING TRANSPARENCY MANIFESTO

## Our Commitment

We believe that hiding pricing is a choice, not an industry requirement. Here's what we commit to:

### 1. Public Baselines
We publish "Starting At" prices for our core services. These aren't fake numbers to get you on the phone—they're real baseline costs for typical engagements.

### 2. Clear Scoping
We'll tell you what's included in baseline pricing and what costs extra. No surprise line items after you sign.

### 3. Exit Clarity
Every proposal includes exit costs and data portability terms. You'll know how to leave before you sign up to stay.

### 4. No Quote Games
You can get a detailed quote without a mandatory "discovery call." We're available to talk, but we don't hold information hostage.

### 5. Competitive Pricing
We're not the cheapest, and we're not trying to be. We're fairly priced for the value delivered. If someone's significantly cheaper, ask what's not included.

## The Transparency Test

When evaluating any MSP (including us), ask:

1. ✅ Can I find any pricing information on your website?
2. ✅ Can I estimate my costs without a sales call?
3. ✅ What are your exit terms and data migration costs?
4. ✅ Are there any hidden fees I should know about?
5. ✅ Can I see a sample contract before committing?

If the answer to any of these is "no," ask yourself: what else aren't they telling you?
