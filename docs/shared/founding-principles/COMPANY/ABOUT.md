## What "Owner-Controlled Infrastructure" Means

An owner-controlled system is one you truly own.

| You Own | You Control | You Can Exit |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| The code | Who has access | Anytime, without permission |
| The credentials | What data goes where | With full documentation |
| The infrastructure | How it scales | Without drama or lock-in |

Ownership is not ideology. It is risk management.

When you own your systems, you:
- Avoid vendor price hikes.
- Keep operating if your vendor disappears.
- Pass security audits with confidence.
- Sleep better at night.

## The Vantus Standard

A system is not complete until it meets all seven criteria:

| Criterion | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Documented** | Architecture, decisions, and runbooks exist and are current. |
| **Secure** | Access, data, and failure modes are designed and tested. |
| **Observable** | You can see, measure, and understand system health. |
| **Recoverable** | Backups, restores, and disasters are proven, not theoretical. |
| **Owned** | You have full control without renegotiation. |
