# STRATEGIC ROADMAP — Vantus Systems (Bootstrapped → Industry Leader)
**Version:** 2.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-05

> This roadmap replaces earlier drafts that had contradictory phase math and inverted pricing tiers.
> It keeps Vantus’s principles (ownership, proof, security, plain language) and aligns sequencing to a bootstrapped reality.

## North Star
Become the **default premium choice** for enterprise-grade websites and web apps for Gulf Coast small businesses — and then expand regionally.

## How we win
1) Productized offers that SMBs understand.
2) Proof artifacts that eliminate buyer fear.
3) Reusable architecture (Vantus Seed) that reduces cycle time.
4) A lightweight internal control plane (VSP-lite) that enforces delivery gates.

---

## Phase 1 — Local Domination (0–12 months)
**Goal:** dominate Ft. Walton Beach + Navarre via a repeatable website+CMS offer.

**What we ship:**
- Package ladder: Website → Website+CMS → Portal (MVP)
- Proof pages (before/after metrics)
- Review flywheel (Google reviews + case studies)

**Targets (directional, not vanity):**
- 20 shipped projects
- 10 case studies
- 50% of new deals from referrals by month 12

---

## Phase 2 — Modular Expansion (12–24 months)
**Goal:** expand from “websites” to repeatable business workflows (portals + integrations) without becoming a custom shop.

**What we ship:**
- Portal MVP modules (auth, RBAC, audit log, one workflow)
- Integration modules (forms → CRM, booking, payments)
- VSP modules for content approvals + change control

**Targets:**
- 50+ total shipped projects
- Documented module catalog (reused across clients)

---

## Phase 3 — Regional Brand (24–48 months)
**Goal:** become the recognizable premium systems shop across the broader Gulf Coast.

**What we ship:**
- Vertical playbooks (medical, legal, trades)
- Partner channel (CPAs, IT providers, agencies)
- Optional Care offering with strict pricing and service boundaries

---

## Guardrails (non-negotiable)
- **Client owns everything.**
- **No content, no launch.**
- **No placeholder pricing in public docs.**
- **Performance and accessibility are gates, not suggestions.**
- **Anything outside scope becomes a change request.**

## Known risks
- Scope creep
- Overbuilding the platform before sales
- Underpricing for premium delivery
