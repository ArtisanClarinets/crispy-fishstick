## I. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This SOP defines the process for performance management and reviews at Vantus Systems. It aims to provide a fair and consistent process for evaluating employee performance, providing feedback, and supporting career development.

## II. KEY PRINCIPLES

- **Fairness:** The performance review process should be fair and unbiased.
- **Transparency:** The criteria for performance evaluation should be transparent.
- **Continuous Feedback:** Feedback should be provided on an ongoing basis, not just during the annual review.
- **Growth-Oriented:** The performance review process should be focused on helping employees grow and develop.

## III. PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE

- **Annual Reviews:** Formal performance reviews are conducted once a year.
- **Quarterly Check-ins:** Informal check-ins are conducted every quarter.
- **360-Degree Feedback:** Feedback is collected from peers, managers, and direct reports.
- **Goal Setting:** Employees and managers work together to set goals for the upcoming year.

## IV. STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

1.  **Self-Assessment:** The employee completes a self-assessment of their performance.
2.  **Manager Assessment:** The manager completes an assessment of the employee's performance.
3.  **360-Degree Feedback:** Collect feedback from peers and direct reports.
4.  **Performance Review Meeting:** The employee and manager meet to discuss the performance review.
5.  **Goal Setting:** The employee and manager work together to set goals for the upcoming year.
6.  **Compensation Review:** The performance review is used as an input into the annual compensation review.

## V. TEMPLATES & CHECKLISTS

- **Self-Assessment Form:** A form for employees to complete their self-assessment.
- **Performance Review Form:** A form for managers to complete their assessment.
- **Goal Setting Form:** A form for setting goals for the upcoming year.

## VI. ESCALATION PATHS

- If an employee disagrees with their performance review, they can escalate it to their manager's manager.
- If the issue cannot be resolved, it can be escalated to the VP of People.
