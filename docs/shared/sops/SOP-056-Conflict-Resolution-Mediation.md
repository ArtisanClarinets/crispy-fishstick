## I. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This SOP defines the process for resolving conflicts and mediating disputes between employees. It aims to provide a fair and confidential process for resolving conflicts in a timely and effective manner.

## II. KEY PRINCIPLES

- **Confidentiality:** All conversations related to conflict resolution are confidential.
- **Neutrality:** The mediator should be a neutral third party.
- **Voluntary:** Mediation is a voluntary process.
- **Respect:** All parties should treat each other with respect.

## III. CONFLICT RESOLUTION PROCESS

- **Informal Resolution:** We encourage employees to try to resolve conflicts informally first.
- **Formal Mediation:** If informal resolution is not possible, an employee can request formal mediation.
- **Investigation:** In some cases, a formal investigation may be necessary.
- **Resolution:** The goal of the process is to reach a resolution that is acceptable to all parties.

## IV. STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

1.  **Request Mediation:** An employee can request mediation by contacting HR.
2.  **Assign a Mediator:** HR will assign a trained mediator to the case.
3.  **Conduct Mediation:** The mediator will meet with both parties to help them resolve the conflict.
4.  **Reach a Resolution:** The mediator will help the parties reach a resolution.
5.  **Document the Resolution:** The resolution will be documented in a written agreement.
6.  **Follow-up:** HR will follow up with the parties to ensure that the resolution is working.

## V. TEMPLATES & CHECKLISTS

- **Mediation Request Form:** A form for requesting mediation.
- **Mediation Agreement Form:** A form for documenting the resolution of a mediation.

## VI. ESCALATION PATHS

- If mediation is not successful, the employee can escalate the issue to the VP of People.
- If the issue involves harassment or discrimination, it will be investigated by HR.
