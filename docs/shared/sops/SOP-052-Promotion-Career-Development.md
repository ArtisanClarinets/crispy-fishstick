## I. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This SOP defines the process for promotions and career development at Vantus Systems. It aims to provide a fair and transparent process for employees to advance their careers.

## II. KEY PRINCIPLES

- **Merit-Based:** Promotions are based on merit, not tenure.
- **Transparency:** The criteria for promotion should be transparent.
- **Equal Opportunity:** All employees should have an equal opportunity to be considered for promotion.
- **Career Growth:** We are committed to helping our employees grow their careers.

## III. PROMOTION PROCESS

- **Promotion Packet:** The employee's manager prepares a promotion packet that highlights the employee's accomplishments.
- **Promotion Committee:** A promotion committee reviews the promotion packet and makes a recommendation.
- **VP Approval:** The VP of the employee's department must approve the promotion.
- **Compensation Adjustment:** The employee's compensation is adjusted to reflect their new role.

## IV. CAREER LADDERS

We have career ladders for all of our major roles. The career ladders define the skills and experience required for each level.

## V. STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

1.  **Identify Promotion Candidates:** Managers identify employees who are ready for promotion.
2.  **Prepare a Promotion Packet:** The manager prepares a promotion packet for the employee.
3.  **Submit the Packet:** The manager submits the promotion packet to the promotion committee.
4.  **Review the Packet:** The promotion committee reviews the packet and makes a recommendation.
5.  **Approve the Promotion:** The VP of the employee's department approves the promotion.
6.  **Announce the Promotion:** The promotion is announced to the company.

## VI. TEMPLATES & CHECKLISTS

- **Promotion Packet Template:** A template for preparing a promotion packet.
- **Career Ladder Documents:** Documents that define the career ladders for each role.

## VII. ESCALATION PATHS

- If an employee feels that they have been unfairly passed over for a promotion, they can escalate it to their manager's manager.
- If the issue cannot be resolved, it can be escalated to the VP of People.
