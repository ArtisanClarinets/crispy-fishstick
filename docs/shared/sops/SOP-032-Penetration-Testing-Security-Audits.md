
# PENETRATION TESTING & SECURITY AUDITS

## I. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This SOP defines the procedures for conducting penetration tests and security audits to assess the security of our systems and identify vulnerabilities. It applies to all production systems.

## II. KEY PRINCIPLES

- **Independence:** Penetration tests and security audits should be conducted by independent third parties.
- **Realism:** The tests should simulate the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) of real-world attackers.
- **Comprehensiveness:** The tests should cover all aspects of the system, from the network to the application layer.
- **Actionability:** The results of the tests should be actionable and used to improve our security posture.

## III. TESTING REQUIREMENTS

- **Annual Penetration Test:** An independent third-party penetration test must be conducted at least once a year.
- **Regular Security Audits:** Internal security audits must be conducted on a regular basis.
- **Scope:** The scope of the tests must be clearly defined and approved by the CISO.
- **Rules of Engagement:** The rules of engagement for the tests must be clearly defined and approved by the CISO.

## IV. STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

1.  **Select a Vendor:** Select a qualified vendor to conduct the penetration test.
2.  **Define the Scope:** Define the scope of the test and the rules of engagement.
3.  **Conduct the Test:** The vendor will conduct the test.
4.  **Receive the Report:** The vendor will provide a report with their findings.
5.  **Triage the Findings:** Triage the findings and prioritize them based on their risk.
6.  **Remediate the Findings:** Remediate the findings within the defined SLAs.
7.  **Verify Remediation:** Verify that the findings have been remediated.

## V. TEMPLATES & CHECKLISTS

- **Penetration Test Scope Template:** A template for defining the scope of a penetration test.
- **Security Audit Checklist:** A checklist of items to look for during a security audit.

## VI. ESCALATION PATHS

- Any critical or high-risk findings from a penetration test or security audit must be immediately reported to the CISO.
- If a finding is not remediated within its SLA, it will be escalated to the VP of Engineering and the CTO.
