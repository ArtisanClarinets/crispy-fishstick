## I. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This SOP defines the procedures for disaster recovery and business continuity to ensure that Vantus Systems can recover from a major disruption and continue to provide services to its clients.

## II. KEY PRINCIPLES

- **Data Integrity:** Ensure that all critical data is backed up and can be restored.
- **Minimize Downtime:** Minimize the time it takes to recover from a disaster.
- **Regular Testing:** Regularly test the disaster recovery plan to ensure it is effective.
- **Clear Communication:** Communicate clearly with all stakeholders during a disaster.

## III. RECOVERY OBJECTIVES

- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO):** The maximum amount of time that a system can be down after a disaster. Our RTO is 4 hours.
- **Recovery Point Objective (RPO):** The maximum amount of data loss that can be tolerated. Our RPO is 1 hour.

## IV. STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE

1.  **Declare a Disaster:** The VP of Operations or CTO can declare a disaster.
2.  **Activate the DR Team:** Activate the Disaster Recovery team.
3.  **Failover to DR Site:** Failover all critical systems to the disaster recovery site.
4.  **Verify System Functionality:** Verify that all systems are functioning correctly at the DR site.
5.  **Communicate with Stakeholders:** Communicate with clients and internal stakeholders about the status of the recovery.
6.  **Failback to Primary Site:** Once the primary site is restored, failback all systems.
7.  **Conduct a Post-Mortem:** Conduct a post-mortem to identify the root cause of the disaster and improve the DR plan.

## V. TEMPLATES & CHECKLISTS

- **Disaster Declaration Checklist:** A checklist for declaring a disaster.
- **DR Test Plan Template:** A template for creating a disaster recovery test plan.

## VI. ESCALATION PATHS

- In the event of a disaster, the VP of Operations is the incident commander.
- The CTO is the executive sponsor for all disaster recovery efforts.
