# CARE_DISCOUNT_GOVERNANCE

**Status:** Active baseline
**Last updated:** 2026-03-07

## Purpose

This document sets discount guardrails for Vantus Care recurring services so pricing stays consistent, margin-aware, and aligned to the enterprise-pack strategy.

## Pricing principle

Care is priced as recurring operational value that follows project delivery.
Discounts must not turn Care into hidden free support for unresolved project work.

## Standard discount guardrails

- Up to 5% discount: seller request with leadership approval
- 6% to 10% discount: written justification plus founder approval
- Above 10%: exceptional case only with explicit executive approval

## Approved discount reasons

- multi-year term commitment,
- strategic expansion within an existing client,
- bundled enterprise-pack purchase with a clear recurring start date,
- competitive replacement with strong expansion potential.

## Non-approved discount reasons

- to absorb work that is actually project work,
- to compensate for missing scope definition,
- to imply guaranteed audit, compliance, or certification outcomes,
- to include free hardware, leasing, or undefined extras.

## Packaging rules

- Project delivery and Care should appear as separate commercial lines.
- Recurring Care discounts must not hide implementation effort.
- If transition-in effort is significant, price it as a one-time line item rather than free onboarding.

## Margin protection checks

Before approving a discount, confirm that:

- labor assumptions still hold,
- support window and response targets still match price,
- included scope remains bounded,
- the discount does not create an expectation of unlimited support.

## Approval workflow

1. Seller documents requested discount, term, package, reason, and expected expansion path.
2. Leadership reviews margin, precedent, and scope risk.
3. Vantus approves, rejects, or revises the request in writing.

## Required quote language

Include language equivalent to the following in any discounted quote:

"Discounted pricing is based on the stated scope, assumptions, and term commitment. Out-of-scope work, project services, and material environment changes may require repricing."

## Renewal guidance

- Introductory discounts should be time-bound and visible.
- Renewals should return to standard pricing unless a new approval is granted.
