## Risk Assessment Criteria

### Severity (Impact if Risk Happens)

| Level | Financial | Operational | Definition |
|-------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Critical** | >$50K | Business stops | Severe damage |
| **High** | $10K-$50K | Major impact | Significant damage |
| **Medium** | $1K-$10K | Limited impact | Moderate damage |
| **Low** | <$1K | Minimal impact | Minor inconvenience |

### Likelihood (Chance of Occurrence)

| Level | Probability | Timeframe |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Almost Certain** | >90% | Likely this month |
| **Likely** | 60-90% | Likely this quarter |
| **Possible** | 30-60% | Could happen this year |
| **Unlikely** | 10-30% | Rare but possible |
| **Rare** | <10% | Almost never |

### Risk Score

Multiply Severity × Likelihood:

| Score | Rating | Action Required |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| 16-25 | 🔴 Critical | Immediate action |
| 9-15 | 🟠 High | Address soon |
| 4-8 | 🟡 Medium | Monitor and plan |
| 1-3 | 🟢 Low | Accept or monitor |

## Quick Risk Assessment Questions

When adding a new risk, ask:

1. What could go wrong?
2. How likely is it?
3. How bad would it be?
4. What can we do about it?
5. Who owns this?
6. When do we need to fix it?
