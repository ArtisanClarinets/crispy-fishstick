## Purpose

This document is an internal benchmarking memo for Vantus Care.
It is meant to support positioning, sales messaging, and planning.
It is not a formal market report, independent ranking, or external publication draft.

## Evidence standard

Use the following evidence labels when updating this memo:

- `Verified public source` - directly supported by a dated public source
- `Internal observation` - based on Vantus review or delivery experience
- `Management estimate` - directional estimate requiring further validation
- `Open question` - claim not yet strong enough to use externally

Do not convert this memo into public-facing copy without source review.

## Benchmarking methodology

This memo compares a small peer set using publicly visible offer signals and internal observations.
The comparison dimensions are:

- pricing transparency,
- ownership and lock-in posture,
- documentation posture,
- security and compliance positioning,
- delivery clarity.

The goal is directional positioning, not a numeric market ranking.

## Directional observations by competitor archetype

### Cortavo-style hardware-bundled model

**Observed pattern**
- Hardware and recurring support can appear commercially bundled.
- Ownership clarity may be weaker for buyers who want clear asset control.

**Vantus directional advantage**
- Vantus can position more clearly around client ownership, portability, and outright purchase.

**Evidence status**
- Ownership-model contrast: high confidence
- Total-cost comparison over time: open question unless sourced case-by-case

### Traditional opaque MSP model

**Observed pattern**
- Public service boundaries and pricing are often less explicit.
- Buyers may need a sales process before understanding fit and cost.

**Vantus directional advantage**
- Vantus has a cleaner public package story and a clearer recurring-scope boundary.

**Evidence status**
- Transparency contrast: medium confidence
- Service quality comparison: open question unless supported by dated source material

### Proprietary-stack or lock-in-oriented model

**Observed pattern**
- Some MSP models appear to lean on tighter stack control and harder exits.

**Vantus directional advantage**
- Vantus can position around ownership, handoff rights, and vendor-neutral transition support.

**Evidence status**
- Exit-readiness contrast: medium confidence
- Competitor lock-in severity: open question unless documented by source or client evidence

### Low-cost local MSP model

**Observed pattern**
- Lower-cost providers may show weaker documentation, reporting depth, or governance language.

**Vantus directional advantage**
- Vantus can position as a more disciplined premium option for buyers who care about support structure and ownership.

**Evidence status**
- Governance contrast: medium confidence
- Security-outcome comparison: open question unless verified

### Security-forward MSP model

**Observed pattern**
- Some providers position heavily around security but use more technical language or less commercially clear packaging.

**Vantus directional advantage**
- Vantus can differentiate with plain-language reporting, ownership, and a clearer recurring-service model.

**Evidence status**
- Messaging clarity contrast: medium confidence
- Security maturity comparison: open question unless measured on a like-for-like basis

## Working positioning conclusions

Based on current review, Vantus Care appears directionally differentiated by:

1. explicit client ownership and no hardware leasing,
2. clearer recurring-service boundaries,
3. stronger handoff and documentation posture,
4. plain-language support positioning,
5. tighter alignment between project delivery and recurring support.

These points are strong enough for internal strategy and careful sales use, but not all are ready for market-superlative external claims.

## Claims to avoid without stronger evidence

Do not externally publish claims such as:

- "only Gulf Coast provider" statements,
- exact market-rank claims,
- unsupported financial market-size numbers,
- precise competitor scorecards,
- quantified performance superiority claims,
- unsupported statements about competitor revenue models or retention.

## Safer external framing

Prefer language like:

- "built around client ownership and portability"
- "designed for clearer scope, pricing, and handoff"
- "positioned for buyers who want recurring support without MSP lock-in"
- "structured to support stronger reporting and operational discipline"

## Open evidence needs

Before stronger publication claims, gather:

- dated screenshots or source links for competitor pricing and service language,
- any public service terms relevant to ownership or lock-in,
- internal source notes for regional observations,
- auditable support metrics if Vantus wants to claim operating superiority.
