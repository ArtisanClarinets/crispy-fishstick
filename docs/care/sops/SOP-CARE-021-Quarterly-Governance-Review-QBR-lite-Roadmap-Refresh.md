## What This Document Is

This SOP holds quarterly reviews to align IT work with your business priorities, risks, and upcoming decisions. Plan ahead to prevent emergencies.

---

## Why This SOP Matters

This SOP defines a repeatable Vantus standard for the service area it covers.
Use it to create consistency, clear ownership, and auditable execution without unsupported market-comparison language.

---

## Why Quarterly Reviews Matter

Regular planning:

- Aligns IT with business goals
- Identifies risks early
- Prevents surprise costs
- Enables proactive decisions
- **Ensures CMMC readiness is maintained** through continuous alignment

---

## Two Core Principles

### 1. Recovery Over Theory

- Quarterly planning reduces emergency work
- Prevention beats reaction
- Schedule beats scramble

### 2. Proof Required

- Roadmap ties to evidence
- Based on observed risks
- Data-driven priorities

## Step-by-Step: Quarterly Review

### Step 1: Prepare Review Document

Using template TPL-CARE-004, gather:

- Last quarter results
- Risk updates
- Roadmap progress
- Budget considerations

### Step 2: Review Last Quarter

Discuss:
| Topic | Questions |
|-------|-----------|
| **What Changed** | Systems, processes, vendors |
| **Incidents** | What happened, how we responded |
| **Improvements** | What we shipped |
| **Misses** | What didn't get done, why |

### Step 3: Refresh 90-Day Roadmap

Update plan with:
| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Priorities** | Top initiatives |
| **Owners** | Who's responsible |
| **Dependencies** | What must happen first |
| **Timelines** | When things happen |

### Step 4: Capture Decisions

Document:

- Decisions made
- Approvals given
- Trade-offs accepted
- Budget approved

### Step 5: Publish and Track

- Finalize QBR notes
- Distribute to stakeholders
- Create backlog items
- Schedule follow-ups

## Real Example

**Scenario**: Warranty expirations prompt lifecycle planning.

**Quarterly Review**:

1. Identify: Firewall warranty expires in 90 days
2. Assess: Risk of no warranty support
3. Present options:
   - Renew warranty
   - Replace with new unit
   - Accept risk
4. Decision: Replace with newer model
5. Roadmap: Replacement project scheduled
6. Budget: Cost approved
7. Follow-up: Project initiated
