---

## Why Fit Checks Matter

A good fit check prevents:

- Buying more service than you need
- Getting less support than you require
- Mismatched expectations
- Wasted time and money
- **CMMC compliance gaps** from misunderstood requirements

---

## Three Core Principles

### 1. Plain Language

- Fit checks use everyday words
- Constraints are clearly stated
- No hidden conditions

### 2. You Stay in Control (Full Ownership Model)

- You own your accounts and data
- We provide guidance and execution
- We clarify what you keep vs. what we manage
- **Exit-ready documentation** from day one
- No proprietary lock-in through service dependencies

### 3. Proof, Not Promises

- We don't promise specific outcomes until we have baseline data
- Claims require measurement
- No guarantees we can't back up

## Step-by-Step: The Fit Check Process

### Step 1: Gather Your Information

We collect details about:

| Category             | What We Ask                                   |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Users**            | How many people need support?                 |
| **Devices**          | Computers, servers, network equipment         |
| **Locations**        | Single office, multiple sites, remote workers |
| **Critical Systems** | What must never go down?                      |
| **Uptime Needs**     | How much downtime can you tolerate?           |
| **Compliance**       | Any industry requirements (HIPAA, PCI, etc.)? |
| **Budget**           | Guardrails for what you can spend             |
| **In-House Skills**  | What can your team handle vs. outsource?      |

### Step 2: Identify Blockers

We flag hard stop issues:

| Red Flag                   | Why It Matters                            |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Refuse required access     | We can't help if we can't see the systems |
| Refuse scope boundaries    | Leads to scope creep and conflict         |
| Unsafe practices           | Some risks we can't accept                |
| Expect free unlimited work | Unrealistic; leads to disappointment      |

### Step 3: Match Services to Needs

- Map your goals to specific modules
- Recommend tier: Foundation, Advanced, or Sovereign
- Document what is **NOT** included (just as important!)

### Step 4: Handle Security Testing Requests

If you want authorized penetration testing:

- We explain requirements: written approval, rules of engagement, stop conditions
- We outline safety constraints
- No testing without proper authorization

### Step 5: Create Fit Summary

We produce:

- One-page Fit Summary
- Preliminary Scope Map draft
- Clear recommendations

### Step 6: Offer Assessment Option

If fit is uncertain, we may suggest:

- Short paid assessment
- Limited-scope evaluation
- Then decide on long-term commitment

### Step 7: Hand Off to Delivery

Sales transfers to Delivery for agreement drafting (SOP-CARE-005).

## Real Example

**Scenario**: Client wants "guaranteed no breaches."

**Our Response**:

1. **Refuse the guarantee**—no one can promise this
2. Offer measurable improvements:
   - Security posture assessment
   - Incident readiness planning
   - Regular monitoring and alerts
3. Set realistic expectations
4. Document what we can and cannot control

## Key Terms Explained

| Term                    | Simple Definition                                                         |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fit Check**           | A structured process to confirm we're the right match for your needs      |
| **Scope**               | The boundaries of what's included in your service                         |
